Patients want to be heard and healthcare providers want to hear them. Yet our frustrations and elations are often lost in the busyness of the medical establishment. Patient experiences are playing a crucial role in healthcare funding around the world.

Natural language processing joins individual experiences into collective sentiments

We present our customers with positive and negative experiences, as well as the story and context connected to them. Bag-of-words sentiment classification allows us to cluster experiences at a particular location and we allow customers to review sentiment and positivity over time.

An artifact to capture spoken experiences

Three simple interactions:Record a positive experience, record a negative experience or ask for information.

Inspiring curiosity: Unique form factor draws people to interact with the Artifacts sharing their space

Hardware features: 3 button interface, infection resistant, integrated speaker and microphone, communicates via wifi, battery powered

The Artifact Team

Our team came together at the Kitchener-Waterloo PCH Hardware Hackathon around a vision of helping people share memories and experiences using collectively-created storytelling objects.
